Student Geek Challenge 2020

Are you interested in programming and/or designing, and are you currently an enrolled student? Then this is the right place for you.

This is your chance to show off your skills and compete against students from all over Europe. You will get the chance to win both a cash prize and a job interview at your closest Novicell office.

All you have to do is solve the case below and upload your solution before the 12th of April 2020.

Please notice that:

  • It’s allowed to solve the case in groups.
  • It’s allowed to use previous material from e.g. school assignments and then adapting it to the case in question.

Case

Build a working commerce implementation. Implement a product, organize products in categories/collections, build a checkout flow (minus payment of course. No phi$hing guys!) We have no special products in mind. Find some fun examples online: T-shirts, hoodies, or maybe a bow tie. You can implement the products and categories/collections in a CMS, pull data from an Open Source API or implement them yourself, it is totally up to you how to get going.

The choice of solution, platform and technology is open. We don’t judge based on specific frameworks or languages. This gives you the freedom to solve the challenge as a website, a smartphone app or something crazy for your TV. Go ahead code something awesome. :)

The development challenge does not require any design or user interface design skills. In this challenge we are evaluating your coding skills, not your design skills. We recommend that you use a free template or copy-paste your layout/UI from somewhere nice.

Optional extras

If you run out of ideas but have time, consider these:

Make a product importer to import products into a new kind of storage and fetch the products from it. Implement single sign on using Facebook or other SoMe. Build a nice search-as-you-type feature. Implement caching of content that doesn’t change very often.

By the way: It is ok to submit a half-finished project. It is a whole lot better to submit something half-finished than submitting nothing. Last time the 2nd place was a half-finished project.
